Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and all the objects that orbit it:
the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids and comets. The
planets fall into two main groups: the rocky inner planets and the gas giant outer planets.

Planets

Name Description Characteristics Image
Mars Mars is a small rocky planet known as the Red Planet because its surface is covered with iron-oxide dust. It has polar ice caps and evidence that liquid water flowed there long ago
  • Distance from the Sun: 227 million km
  • Rotation (day): 24.6 hours
  • Number of moons: 2, Phobos and Deimos
  • Atmosphere: Present but very thin (mainly carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and argon gases)
Jupiter Jupiter is a world of extremes. It's the largest planet in our solar system, if it were a hollow shell, 1,000 Earths could fit inside. It's also the oldest planet, forming from the dust and gases left over from the Sun's formation 4.6 billion years ago, But it has the shortest day in the solar system.
  • Distance from the Sun: 778 million km
  • Rotation (day): 9.9 hours
  • Number of moons: 95(officially recognized by the International Astronomical Union). The four largest moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto
  • Atmosphere: Present (thick hydrogen & helium)
Saturn Saturn is the second-largest planet and is most famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock. Like Jupiter it is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium.
  • Distance from the Sun: 1.4 billion kilometers
  • Rotation (day): 10.7 hours
  • Number of moons: 146 moons
  • Atmosphere: Present (mainly hydrogen & helium)
